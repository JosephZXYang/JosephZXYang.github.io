CS 4780 Lecture 11: Model Selection

Posted on 2018-12-01 | In CS 4780 Study Notes

Make Sure Your Model is Optimally Tuned

Recall ERM: find $w$ such that $$ \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n}l_{s}(h_{w}(x_{i}), y_{i}) + \lambda \cdot r(w)$$ is minimized.

The first term is the loss and the second term is the regularizer.

Underfitting and Overfitting

  • Underfitting: the classifier is not expressive enough to even account for the training set. In other words, the classifier is almost meaningless.
    • Training error: high
    • Testing error: high
  • Overfitting: the classifier learned from the training set is too specific and could only work on the training set.
    • Training error: almost 0
    • Testing error: high

Identify the Sweet Point

Divide the given data set into training set and validation set. Try multiple $\lambda$ on the validation set.

K-fold cross validation

Divide the data into $k$ subsets. Train on $k-1$ of them and validate on the rest one. Iterate $k$ times and take the average of $k$ validation errors as the final validation error.

This trick is very effective if the data set is small and you canâ€™t afford losing data for pure validation use.

Telescopic search

First find the best order of $\lambda$, then try different values of that order. Keep iterating such way.

Early stopping

Stop iterating even if the optimization has not converged yet.

As $\lambda$ gets larger, bias goes up and variance goes down.
