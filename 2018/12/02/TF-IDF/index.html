TF-IDF

Posted on 2018-12-02 | In Study

Intro

TF-IDF, abbreviation for term frequency-inverse document frequency, is a method to reflect the importance of a word to a document in a collection of documents.

The tf-idf value of a word is proportional to how many times it appears in a document and is offset by how many times it appears in the whole collection in total. The idea is as follows:

Term Frequency

Suppose we want to find out a document that is most relevant to our key word “I love you”. First, we would like to eliminate all documents that don’t contain the three key words at all. However, still a lot documents may contain the three words. Then we would like to find the documents with most occurrences of our key words. Such documents would more likely to be willing results (documents most related to “I love you”).

The weight of a term in a document is proportional to its occurrence in the document (or term frequency).

Inverse Document Frequency

However, there are some words that may appear many times in every document in our collection (for example, pronouns or articles). In this case, if two document contain “I love you”, one with 1000 occurrences of ‘I’ and 1 occurrence of ‘love you’, the other with 30 occurrences of ‘I love you’, without any adjustment, our algorithm would rank the first one as most related, which is not our intuition. Hence an inverse document factor is incorporated in order to diminish the impact of frequent words all throught the document collection.

The specificity of a term can be quantified as an inverse function of the number of documents in which it occurs.
